Dogme 95

Dogme 95 (1995-2005) was a Danish avant-garde filmmaking movement founded by Lars von Trier and Thomas Vinterberg. It aimed to "purify" cinema by adhering to strict rules outlined in the "Vow of Chastity," rejecting special effects and focusing on raw storytelling and performances.
