Robert Wiene

Birth: 27 April 1873, Breslau, Silesia, German Empire

Notable Works: The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari (1920), INRI (1923)

obert Wiene (German: [ˈviːnə]; 27 April 1873 – 17 July 1938) was a German film director, screenwriter and producer, active during the silent era. He is widely-known for directing the landmark 1920 film The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari and a succession of other expressionist films. Wiene also directed a variety of other films of varying styles and genres. Following the Nazi rise to power in Germany, Wiene, who was of Jewish descent, fled into exile.

Note: Only about 20 of the more than 90 movies in which Robert Wiene collaborated still exist.
