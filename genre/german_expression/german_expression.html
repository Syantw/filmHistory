German Expressionism

German Expressionism (1910s-1930s) emerged in the aftermath of World War I, characterized by its distorted visuals, stark contrasts, and exploration of psychological themes. This cinematic movement used exaggerated sets and lighting to reflect inner turmoil, influencing horror and noir genres.

Directors

Fritz Lang

Known for Metropolis (1927) and M (1931), Lang blended futuristic visions with social critique.
